Explainer

What is a "helicopter parent"?

The name comes from a helicopter that hovers (flies in one place) over something. A helicopter parent always "hovers" over their child — watching everything, worrying about everything, doing everything for them.

Overprotective

They try to protect you from every little bad thing — even things that aren't really dangerous.

Always watching

They want to know where you are, what you're doing, and who you're with — all the time.

Doing things for you

They jump in to fix problems or do tasks that you could handle yourself.

It comes from love

They usually do it because they love you SO much — they just worry too much sometimes!

Warning Signs

Signs your parents might be helicopter parents

Read through these and see if any sound familiar!

Having 1–2 of these is totally normal. If you're nodding at most of them, your parents might have helicopter tendencies!
1

They text or call you constantly when you're out — and get worried if you don't reply immediately.

2

They pack your school bag, do parts of your homework, or check all your schoolwork.

3

When you have a fight with a friend, they want to call that friend's parents straight away.

4

They track your phone location and know where you are at every moment.

5

They still pick out your clothes, food, or activities — even though you're old enough to decide.

6

They get very upset or anxious when you want to do something independently (like going to a friend's house alone).

7

They plan every single hour of your free time — you almost never get to just be bored.

8

If you get a bad grade, they get more upset about it than you do.

9

If you forget your lunch or sports kit, they bring it to school for you straight away.

10

They speak for you in situations where you could speak for yourself — like talking to a teacher or ordering food.

Your Experience

How does it feel to have helicopter parents?

You're not alone. Lots of kids have these mixed feelings — and all of them make sense.

Frustrating things

Feeling like they don't trust you
No privacy — they want to know everything
Never getting to figure things out yourself
Feeling nervous about doing anything alone

Confusing feelings

Knowing they do it because they love you
Not sure if you should feel grateful or annoyed
Feeling embarrassed in front of friends
Sometimes wishing they'd back off

Tricky effects

Feeling less confident in yourself
Not practising how to solve your own problems
Struggling more when parents aren't around
Feeling more anxious, not less

What's actually okay

Feeling all of the above is totally normal
It's okay to wish things were different
You can learn independence little by little
Talking about it can really help
